Global Young Adult Literacy Rates

Literacy is a common marker for development in country's populations. It varies from country to country and from year to year. Many people in first world countries assume that most countries have high literacies. The discrepancies between countries' literacies are not often talked about. The following graphs examine the literacy rates in three different countries from three different regions of the globe. The information specifically looks at populations aged 15-24 years old.

The three countries chosen come from the Middle East, Africa, and Europe. These countries are different in many ways, with their societies functioning differently. The data reflects this.

As you can see, the common trend for young adult literacy is to increase as you move towards more recent years. This can be accredited to higher accesibility to the internet. The dissemination of information across the net has allowed more people in more places to learn.

The following graph takes another factor into account: gender. The wage gap is a hot topic right now in gender politics. There is an education gap as well in certain countries where women's rights are not prioritized. It is interesting to not only see the discrepancies between countries but also between compatriot genders.
